Electric Flux (\(\Phi_E\))

Equation:

$$\Phi_E = \int \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A}$$

Variables:

  • \(\Phi_E\): Electric flux [Vm]
  • \(\vec{E}\): Electric field vector [N/C or V/m]
  • \(d\vec{A}\): Differential area vector [mÂ²]
This equation calculates the electric flux through a surface. It represents the amount of electric field passing through a given area, accounting for the angle between the field lines and the surface.
