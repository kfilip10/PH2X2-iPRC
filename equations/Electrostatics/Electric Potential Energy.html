Electrostatic Potential Energy (\(U_E\))

Equation:

$$U_E = k \frac{q_1 q_2}{r}$$

Variables:

  • \(U_E\): Electrostatic potential energy [J]
  • \(k\): Coulomb's constant \((8.99 \times 10^9 \, \text{N m}^2/\text{C}^2)\)
  • \(q_1\): Charge of the first particle [C]
  • \(q_2\): Charge of the second particle [C]
  • \(r\): Distance between the two charges [m]
This equation represents the electrostatic potential energy between two point charges. It describes how energy is stored in the configuration of the charges due to their electrostatic interaction.
