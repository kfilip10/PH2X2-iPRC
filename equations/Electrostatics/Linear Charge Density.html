Linear Charge Density (\(\lambda\))

Equation:

$$\lambda = \frac{Q}{L}$$

Variables:

  • \(\lambda\): Linear charge density [C/m]
  • \(Q\): Total charge [C]
  • \(L\): Length over which the charge is distributed [m]
This equation defines the linear charge density, which is the amount of electric charge per unit length along an object. It is useful in understanding the distribution of charge in linear conductors or charged lines.
