Coulomb's Law

Equation:

$$|\vec{ F}_{12} | = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{| q_1 | | q_2 |}{r_{12}^2}$$

Variables:

  • \(|\vec{ F}_{12} |\): Magnitude of the electrostatic force between two point charges [N]
  • \(q_1\): Magnitude of the first point charge [C]
  • \(q_2\): Magnitude of the second point charge [C]
  • \(r_{12}\): Distance between the two point charges [m]
  • \(\epsilon_0\): Permittivity of free space [F/m]
This equation calculates the magnitude of the electrostatic force between two point charges in a vacuum. It is based on the inverse square law and is central to electrostatic interactions.
