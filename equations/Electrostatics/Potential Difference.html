Electric Potential Difference and Electric Field

Equation:

$$V_f - V_i = -\int_i^f \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l}$$

Variables:

  • \(V_f\): Final electric potential [V]
  • \(V_i\): Initial electric potential [V]
  • \(\vec{E}\): Electric field vector [N/C or V/m]
  • \(d\vec{l}\): Differential path length vector [m]
This equation relates the change in electric potential between two points to the line integral of the electric field along a path connecting them. It is a fundamental concept in electromagnetism, illustrating how potentials are influenced by electric fields.
