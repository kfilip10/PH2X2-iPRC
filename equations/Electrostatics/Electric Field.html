Electric Field (\(\vec{E}\))

Equation:

$$\vec{E} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2} \hat{r}$$

Variables:

  • \(\vec{E}\): Electric field vector [N/C]
  • \(q\): Point charge [C]
  • \(\epsilon_0\): Permittivity of free space [C²/(N·m²) or F/m]
  • \(r\): Distance from the charge to the point of interest [m]
  • \(\hat{r}\): Unit vector in the direction from the charge to the point of interest
This equation describes the electric field generated by a point charge in space. The field strength decreases with the square of the distance from the charge and is directed radially outward from the charge.
