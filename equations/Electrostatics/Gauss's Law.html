Gauss's Law

Equation:

$$\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_\text{enc}}{\epsilon_0}$$

Variables:

  • \(\vec{E}\): Electric field [N/C]
  • \(d\vec{A}\): Differential area vector [mÂ²]
  • \(q_\text{enc}\): Charge enclosed [C]
  • \(\epsilon_0\): Permittivity of free space [F/m]
Gauss's Law relates the electric flux passing through a closed surface to the charge enclosed by that surface. It is a fundamental principle in electromagnetism.
