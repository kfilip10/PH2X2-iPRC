Electric Field Contribution (dE)

Equation:

$$dE = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{dq}{r^2} \hat{r}$$

Variables:

  • \(dE\): Differential electric field contribution [N/C]
  • \(\epsilon_0\): Permittivity of free space [\(\frac{C^2}{Nm^2}\)]
  • \(dq\): Differential charge [C]
  • \(r\): Distance from charge to point of interest [m]
  • \(\hat{r}\): Unit vector pointing from the charge to the point of interest
This equation calculates the contribution to the electric field from a differential charge element at a distance \(r\) from the point of interest, pointing in the direction of the unit vector \(\hat{r}\).
