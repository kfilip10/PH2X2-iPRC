Magnetic Field (|\(\vec{B}\)|)

Equation:

$$|\vec{B}| = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$

Variables:

  • \(|\vec{B}|\): Magnetic field strength [T]
  • \(\mu_0\): Permeability of free space [TÂ·m/A]
  • \(I\): Current through the wire [A]
  • \(r\): Radial distance from the wire [m]
This equation calculates the magnitude of the magnetic field created by a long, straight current-carrying wire at a certain distance. It demonstrates that the magnetic field strength is directly proportional to the current and inversely proportional to the distance from the wire.
