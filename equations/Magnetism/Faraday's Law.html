Electromotive Force (EMF)

Equation:

$$\mathcal{E} = -N \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$$

Variables:

  • \(\mathcal{E}\): Electromotive force (EMF) [V]
  • \(N\): Number of turns in the coil
  • \(\Phi_B\): Magnetic flux [Wb]
  • \(t\): Time [s]
This equation represents Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction, which states that the electromotive force induced in a circuit is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux through the circuit. The negative sign indicates the direction of the induced EMF, as described by Lenz's Law.
