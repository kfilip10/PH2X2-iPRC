Magnetic Field Inside a Solenoid

Equation:

$$| \vec{B} | = \frac{\mu_0 I N}{l}$$

Variables:

  • \(| \vec{B} |\): Magnitude of the magnetic field inside the solenoid [T]
  • \(\mu_0\): Permeability of free space [TÂ·m/A]
  • \(I\): Current through the solenoid [A]
  • \(N\): Number of turns in the solenoid
  • \(l\): Length of the solenoid [m]
This equation calculates the magnitude of the magnetic field inside a long solenoid, assuming it is uniformly wound and the solenoid is much longer than its diameter.
