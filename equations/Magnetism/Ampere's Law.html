Ampère's Law

Equation:

$$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I$$

Variables:

  • \(\vec{B}\): Magnetic field vector [T]
  • \(d\vec{l}\): Infinitesimal length vector along the closed loop [m]
  • \(\mu_0\): Permeability of free space \((4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A})\)
  • \(I\): Current enclosed by the loop [A]
Ampère's Law relates the integrated magnetic field around a closed loop to the electric current passing through the loop. It is fundamental in understanding how magnetic fields are generated by currents.
