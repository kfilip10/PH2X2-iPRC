Magnetic Field (Biot-Savart Law)

Equation:

$$\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \int \frac{I d\vec{l} \times \hat{r}}{r^2}$$

Variables:

  • \(\vec{B}\): Magnetic field [T]
  • \(\mu_0\): Permeability of free space [TÂ·m/A]
  • \(I\): Current [A]
  • \(d\vec{l}\): Infinitesimal element of the current-carrying wire [m]
  • \(\hat{r}\): Unit vector pointing from the wire element to the point of interest
  • \(r\): Distance from the wire element to the point of interest [m]
This equation, known as the Biot-Savart Law, describes the magnetic field generated by a steady current. It integrates contributions from all infinitesimal segments of a current-carrying conductor.
