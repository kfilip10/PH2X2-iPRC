Magnetic Flux (\(\Phi_B\))

Equation:

$$\Phi_B = \int \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A}$$

Variables:

  • \(\Phi_B\): Magnetic flux [Wb (Weber)]
  • \(\vec{B}\): Magnetic field vector [T (Tesla)]
  • \(d\vec{A}\): Differential area vector, perpendicular to the surface [mÂ²]
This equation represents the magnetic flux through a surface, which is the integral of the magnetic field over that surface. It illustrates how the field lines penetrate the given area.
