Magnetic Force on a Current-Carrying Wire

Equation:

$$\vec{F}_B = I\vec{l} \times \vec{B}$$

Variables:

  • \(\vec{F}_B\): Magnetic force on the wire [N]
  • \(I\): Current through the wire [A]
  • \(\vec{l}\): Length vector of the wire [m]
  • \(\vec{B}\): Magnetic field vector [T]
This equation represents the magnetic force experienced by a wire carrying an electric current in the presence of a magnetic field. The force is determined by the cross product of the current and length vector with the magnetic field vector.
