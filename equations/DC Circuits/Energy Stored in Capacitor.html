Electrostatic Potential Energy in a Capacitor

Equation:

$$U_C = \frac{Q^2}{2C}$$

Variables:

  • \(U_C\): Electrostatic potential energy stored in the capacitor [J]
  • \(Q\): Charge stored in the capacitor [C]
  • \(C\): Capacitance of the capacitor [F]
This equation describes the energy stored in a capacitor as a function of the charge stored and the capacitance. It is applicable when considering electrical potential energy storage in capacitive elements.
