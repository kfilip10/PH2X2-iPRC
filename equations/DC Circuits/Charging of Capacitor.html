Charge on a Capacitor Over Time

Equation:

$$q(t) = C E \left( 1 - e^{-t / \tau_c} \right)$$

Variables:

  • \(q(t)\): Charge on the capacitor at time \(t\) [C]
  • \(C\): Capacitance [F]
  • \(E\): Electric field or electromotive force [V]
  • \(t\): Time [s]
  • \(\tau_c\): Time constant, \(\tau_c = RC\) [s]
  • \(R\): Resistance [Î©]
This equation describes the charge \(q(t)\) on a capacitor, which increases over time when the capacitor is connected to a voltage source through a resistor. The charge approaches its maximum value asymptotically as time \(t\) progresses, characterized by the time constant \(\tau_c\).
