Current in a Conductor (I)

Equation:

$$I(t) = \frac{dQ}{dt}$$

Variables:

  • \(I(t)\): Current at time \(t\) [A]
  • \(Q\): Electric charge [C]
  • \(t\): Time [s]
This equation describes the current in a conductor as the rate of change of electric charge over time. It is fundamental in understanding how charge flows in electrical circuits.
