Kirchhoff's Junction Rule

Equation:

$$\sum I_\text{in} = \sum I_\text{out}$$

Variables:

  • \(\sum I_\text{in}\): Total current entering the junction [A]
  • \(\sum I_\text{out}\): Total current leaving the junction [A]
This equation, known as Kirchhoff's Junction Rule, states that the total current entering a junction in an electrical circuit is equal to the total current leaving the junction. This principle is based on the conservation of electric charge.
