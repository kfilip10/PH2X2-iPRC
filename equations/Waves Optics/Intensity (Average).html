Intensity of Electromagnetic Wave (I)

Equation:

$$I = \frac{E_0 B_0}{2\mu_0}$$

Variables:

  • \(I\): Intensity of the electromagnetic wave [W/mÂ²]
  • \(E_0\): Peak electric field [V/m]
  • \(B_0\): Peak magnetic field [T]
  • \(\mu_0\): Permeability of free space [H/m]
This equation describes the intensity of an electromagnetic wave given the peak values of the electric and magnetic fields in free space. This is also known as the average Poynting vector.
