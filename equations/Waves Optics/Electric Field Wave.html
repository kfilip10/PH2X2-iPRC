Electromagnetic Wave Equation

Equation:

$$E_y(x, t) = E_0 \sin(kx \pm \omega t + \phi)$$

Variables:

  • \(E_y(x, t)\): Electric field at position \(x\) and time \(t\) [V/m]
  • \(E_0\): Amplitude of the wave [V/m]
  • \(k\): Wave number [rad/m]
  • \(\omega\): Angular frequency [rad/s]
  • \(t\): Time [s]
  • \(\phi\): Phase constant [rad]
This equation represents the electric field component of an electromagnetic wave propagating in one dimension. The wave can travel in either the positive or negative x-direction, as indicated by the \(\pm\) sign.
