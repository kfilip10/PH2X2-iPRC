Equation:

$$\theta_R = 1.22 \frac{\lambda}{d}$$

Variables:

  • \(\theta_R\): Angular resolution [radians]
  • \(\lambda\): Wavelength of light [m]
  • \(d\): Diameter of the aperture [m]
This equation describes the Rayleigh criterion for the resolvable angle of light. It determines the minimum angular separation at which two point light sources can be distinctly resolved through an optical instrument.
