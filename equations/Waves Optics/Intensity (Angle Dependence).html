Intensity of Polarized Light

Equation:

$$I = I_0 \cos^2 \theta$$

Variables:

  • \(I\): Intensity of the light after passing through the polarizer [W/m²]
  • \(I_0\): Initial intensity of the light [W/m²]
  • \(\theta\): Angle between the light's initial polarization direction and the axis of the polarizer [degrees]
This equation describes the intensity of light after passing through a polarizer. It demonstrates that the intensity is proportional to the square of the cosine of the angle between the light's initial polarization direction and the polarizer's axis.
