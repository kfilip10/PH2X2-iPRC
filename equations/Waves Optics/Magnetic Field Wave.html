Electromagnetic Wave (B-field)

Equation:

$$B_z(x, t) = B_0 \sin(kx \pm \omega t + \phi)$$

Variables:

  • \(B_z\): Magnetic field at position x and time t [T]
  • \(B_0\): Amplitude of the magnetic field [T]
  • \(k\): Wave number [rad/m]
  • \(\omega\): Angular frequency [rad/s]
  • \(t\): Time [s]
  • \(\phi\): Phase constant [rad]
  • \(x\): Position along the wave direction [m]
This equation describes the behavior of the magnetic field component of a sinusoidal electromagnetic wave traveling in space. The wave can propagate forward or backward, indicated by the \(\pm\) symbol.
