Refractive Index (n)

Equation:

$$n = \frac{c}{v}$$

Variables:

  • \(n\): Index of refraction [unitless]
  • \(c\): Speed of light in a vacuum [m/s]
  • \(v\): Speed of light in the medium [m/s]
This equation describes the refractive index of a medium, defined as the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum to the speed of light in the medium. The refractive index indicates how much the light is bent, or refracted, when entering the medium.
