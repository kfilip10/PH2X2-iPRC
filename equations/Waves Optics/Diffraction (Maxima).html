Diffraction Grating Equation

Equation:

$$d \sin \theta = m \lambda$$

Variables:

  • \(d\): Distance between slits [m]
  • \(\theta\): Angle of diffraction [radians or degrees]
  • \(m\): Order of the diffraction maximum (integer)
  • \(\lambda\): Wavelength of the light [m]
This equation describes the condition for constructive interference in a diffraction grating, where light waves passing through multiple slits interfere and produce maxima at specific angles.
