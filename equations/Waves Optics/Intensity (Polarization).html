Intensity of Initially Unpolarized Light

Equation:

$$I = I_0 \frac{1}{2}$$

Variables:

  • \(I\): Reduced current intensity [A]
  • \(I_0\): Initial current intensity [A]
This equation represents the reduction of an initial current intensity to half of its original value. This can occur, for example, when passing through a component with specific resistive characteristics in a circuit.
