Diffraction Grating Equation

Equation:

$$d \sin \theta = (m + \frac{1}{2}) \lambda$$

Variables:

  • \(d\): Distance between adjacent slits in the grating [m]
  • \(\theta\): Angle of diffraction [radians]
  • \(m\): Order of the maximum (m is an integer)
  • \(\lambda\): Wavelength of light [m]
This equation describes the condition for maxima in a diffraction pattern produced when light passes through a diffraction grating. The equation calculates the angles at which light of a particular wavelength is constructively interfered.
