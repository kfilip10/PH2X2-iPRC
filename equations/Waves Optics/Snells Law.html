Snell's Law

Equation:

$$n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2$$

Variables:

  • \(n_1\): Refractive index of the first medium
  • \(\theta_1\): Angle of incidence [degrees or radians]
  • \(n_2\): Refractive index of the second medium
  • \(\theta_2\): Angle of refraction [degrees or radians]
Snell's Law describes the relationship between the angles and refractive indices of light as it passes from one medium to another. It is used to determine how much the path of light is bent, or refracted, when entering a different medium.
