Poynting Vector (S)

Equation:

$$S = \frac{1}{\mu_0} \vec{E} \times \vec{B}$$

Variables:

  • \(S\): Poynting vector [W/mÂ²]
  • \(\mu_0\): Permeability of free space [H/m]
  • \(\vec{E}\): Electric field vector [V/m]
  • \(\vec{B}\): Magnetic field vector [T]
This equation represents the Poynting vector, which describes the power per unit area carried by an electromagnetic wave. It is the cross product of the electric field and magnetic field vectors, scaled by the inverse of the permeability of free space.
