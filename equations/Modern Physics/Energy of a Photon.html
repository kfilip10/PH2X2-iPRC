Energy of a Photon

Equation:

$$E = hf$$

Variables:

  • \(E\): Energy of the photon [J]
  • \(h\): Planck's constant \((6.626 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J}\cdot\text{s})\)
  • \(f\): Frequency of the electromagnetic wave [Hz]
This equation represents the energy of a photon, which is proportional to the frequency of the electromagnetic wave. Planck's constant is a fundamental constant used to describe the behavior of particles at the quantum level.
