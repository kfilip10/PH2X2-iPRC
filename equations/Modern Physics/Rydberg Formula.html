Rydberg Formula for Hydrogen Spectrum

Equation:

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = \frac{E_0}{hc} \left( \frac{1}{n_i^2} - \frac{1}{n_f^2} \right)$$

Variables:

  • \(\lambda\): Wavelength of emitted or absorbed light [m]
  • \(E_0\): Rydberg energy constant [J]
  • \(h\): Planck's constant [JÂ·s]
  • \(c\): Speed of light in vacuum [m/s]
  • \(n_i\): Initial principal quantum number (positive integer)
  • \(n_f\): Final principal quantum number (positive integer)
This equation represents the Rydberg formula, which predicts the wavelength of light resulting from an electron moving between energy levels in a hydrogen atom.
