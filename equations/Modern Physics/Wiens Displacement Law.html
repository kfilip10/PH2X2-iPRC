Wien's Displacement Law

Equation:

$$\lambda_\text{max} T = 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m} \cdot \text{K}$$

Variables:

  • \(\lambda_\text{max}\): Wavelength of maximum emission [m]
  • \(T\): Absolute temperature of the black body [K]
This equation relates the wavelength at which the emission of a black body spectrum is maximized to its temperature. It is crucial in understanding how the emission spectrum shifts with temperature.
