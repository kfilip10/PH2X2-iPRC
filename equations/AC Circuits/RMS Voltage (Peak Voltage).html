Root Mean Square Voltage (Vrms)

Equation:

$$V_\text{rms} = \frac{V_0}{\sqrt{2}}$$

Variables:

  • \(V_\text{rms}\): Root mean square voltage [V]
  • \(V_0\): Peak voltage [V]
This equation computes the root mean square (RMS) voltage of an alternating current (AC) circuit. It is useful for determining the effective voltage that results in the same power dissipation as a direct current (DC) circuit.
