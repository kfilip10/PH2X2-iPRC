Inductance of a Solenoid (L)

Equation:

$$L = \frac{\mu_0 N^2 A}{l}$$

Variables:

  • \(L\): Inductance [Henrys]
  • \(\mu_0\): Permeability of free space [T·m/A]
  • \(N\): Number of turns per unit length [turns/m]
  • \(A\): Cross-sectional area of the solenoid [m²]
  • \(l\): Length of the solenoid [m]
This equation calculates the inductance of a solenoid, which is a coil of wire designed to create a magnetic field when an electric current passes through it. The inductance depends on the permeability of free space, the number of turns, the cross-sectional area, and the length of the solenoid.
