Average Power in AC Circuits

Equation:

$$P_\text{avg} = V_\text{rms} I_\text{rms} \cos \phi$$

Variables:

  • \(P_\text{avg}\): Average power [W]
  • \(V_\text{rms}\): Root mean square voltage [V]
  • \(I_\text{rms}\): Root mean square current [A]
  • \(\phi\): Phase angle between current and voltage [rad]
This equation calculates the average power delivered to an AC circuit, considering the root mean square (RMS) values of voltage and current and the phase difference between them.
