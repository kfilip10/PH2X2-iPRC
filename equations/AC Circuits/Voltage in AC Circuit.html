AC Voltage as a Function of Time

Equation:

$$v(t) = v_0 \sin(\omega t + \phi)$$

Variables:

  • \(v(t)\): Instantaneous voltage [V]
  • \(v_0\): Peak voltage [V]
  • \(\omega\): Angular frequency [rad/s]
  • \(t\): Time [s]
  • \(\phi\): Phase constant of wave [radians]
This equation describes the instantaneous voltage of an alternating current (AC) as a sinusoidal function of time, characterized by a peak voltage, angular frequency, and phase constant.
