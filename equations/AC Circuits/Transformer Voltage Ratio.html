Transformer Voltage Ratio

Equation:

$$\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}$$

Variables:

  • \(V_s\): Secondary voltage [V]
  • \(V_p\): Primary voltage [V]
  • \(N_s\): Number of turns in the secondary coil
  • \(N_p\): Number of turns in the primary coil
This equation represents the relationship between the voltages across the primary and secondary coils of a transformer and the number of turns in each coil. It is fundamental in understanding how transformers step up or step down voltage levels.
