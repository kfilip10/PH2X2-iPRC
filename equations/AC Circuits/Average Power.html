Average Power in a Resistor

Equation:

$$P_\text{avg} = I_\text{rms}^2 R$$

Variables:

  • \(P_\text{avg}\): Average power dissipated in the resistor [W]
  • \(I_\text{rms}\): Root mean square current [A]
  • \(R\): Resistance [Î©]
This equation calculates the average power dissipated in a resistor due to an alternating current, using the root mean square value of the current.
