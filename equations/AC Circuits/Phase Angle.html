Phase Constant (\(\phi\))

Equation:

$$\phi = \tan^{-1} \frac{X_L - X_C}{R}$$

Variables:

  • \(X_L\): Inductive reactance [Ω]
  • \(X_C\): Capacitive reactance [Ω]
  • \(R\): Resistance [Ω]
  • \(\phi\): Phase Constant [radians]
This equation calculates the phase Constant in an AC circuit. The phase Constant indicates the phase difference between the voltage and the current due to the presence of reactive components such as inductors and capacitors.
