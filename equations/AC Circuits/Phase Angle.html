Phase Angle (\(\phi\))

Equation:

$$\phi = \tan^{-1} \frac{X_L - X_C}{R}$$

Variables:

  • \(X_L\): Inductive reactance [Ω]
  • \(X_C\): Capacitive reactance [Ω]
  • \(R\): Resistance [Ω]
  • \(\phi\): Phase angle [radians]
This equation calculates the phase angle in an AC circuit. The phase angle indicates the phase difference between the voltage and the current due to the presence of reactive components such as inductors and capacitors.
