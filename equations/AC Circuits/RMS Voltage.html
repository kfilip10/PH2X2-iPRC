RMS Voltage in AC Circuits

Equation:

$$V_\text{rms} = I_\text{rms} Z$$

Variables:

  • \(V_\text{rms}\): Root mean square voltage [V]
  • \(I_\text{rms}\): Root mean square current [A]
  • \(Z\): Impedance [Î©]
This equation relates the root mean square (RMS) voltage across a circuit to the RMS current through it and the impedance of the circuit. It is fundamental in analyzing AC circuits.
