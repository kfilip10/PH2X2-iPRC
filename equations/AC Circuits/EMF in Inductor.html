Induced EMF in an Inductor

Equation:

$$\mathcal{E}_L = -L \frac{dI}{dt}$$

Variables:

  • \(\mathcal{E}_L\): Induced electromotive force (EMF) [V]
  • \(L\): Inductance [H]
  • \(\frac{dI}{dt}\): Rate of change of current [A/s]
This equation describes the electromotive force induced in an inductor as a result of the rate of change of current through it. The negative sign indicates the direction of the induced EMF opposes the change in current, in accordance with Lenz's law.
